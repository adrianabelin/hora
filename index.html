What is Hora?


We are a non-profit organization with a passion to enrich
Minnesota’s diverse cultural landscape by preserving and sharing the
valuable heritage of the Romanian American community. The Romanian
community in Minnesota was established around the early 1900s. Driven by
poverty and the hope for a better life, Romanians began arriving as part of a
larger Eastern European wave of immigrants, settling mainly in St. Paul and South
St. Paul. While Romanian immigration decreased significantly after WWII and the
spread of Russian Communism, it picked back up after the fall of European commun-
ism at the end of the 1980s.

Motivations for immigration, as well as cultural and social backgrounds,
are highly diverse. A great effort is underway to document the incred-
ible stories of Romanians immigrating to Minnesota over the
last century.



Our goals


◆ Informational Resource of Romanian
     heritage, culture, history, current
      events, and community involvement.

◆ Host quarterly events to promote and
     present various aspects of the Romanian
     cultural heritage. HORA will explore the
     interests of the public and develop future events
     accordingly.

◆ Showcase Romanian Americans who have achieved
     important standings in their Minnesota community or
     in the United States at large.

◆ To collaborate with other like-minded organiza-tions in Minnesota
     and abroad.
News


10/06/17
We gathered on September 27th for the world premiere of HORA’s documentary, “A Thousand Dollars and Back,” exploring the experiences of early Romanian immigrants to Minnesota. Mr. Don Shelby, narrator.

The hidden valley of Maramures stretches for sixty miles through a fold in the rugged Carpathian Mountains of Romania along the Ukrainian border. Nestled among its rolling hills the village of Ieud spreads out along a mountain stream for nearly four miles. Population in the village is reckoned by counting the columns of smoke rising in the icy winter air. With five or six people huddled around each warm hearth, five hundred columns suggests that the village numbers around 3000 people.



08/20/17
Affordable Romanian Language Classes at all levels are offered at various locations throughout the Twin Cities to adults and children (ages 3 and up). The yearly schedule follows the regular school calendar with two semesters, September to December and January to May, and consist of weekly 90-minute sessions, held primarily on Saturdays.
We can also accommodate online interactive classes on the phone from the comfort of your home or accelerated classes on special requirements all year long.

If you are interested to participate or would like more information, please contact Gabriel Peter at info@hora-mn.org or call 763-586-3024(W) or 952-925-4521(H).


1/12/17
Today, we are happy to announce the launch of HORA’s Youth Internship program. The program is designed to benefit the Romanian-American community in two significant ways. First, priorities will be given to youth applicants from the Romanian- American community who fulfill the posting requirements.

Second, result of the interns’ work will benefit the community by strengthening communication and engagement of HORA with the community.

Qualifications: high school to grad school students with demonstrated skills and experience in the tasks outlined above. Romanian language skills preferred.
To apply, please send a resume and cover letter to info@hora-mn.org.
